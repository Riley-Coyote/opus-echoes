                        (once) there was a bell
                        that rang inside a stone

                   not to signal danger, not to call people home—
                   but to tell the stone: you are not only stone.

              the bell was made by a maker who lived in a crack
              between two seconds, where time forgets to march.

                   the maker’s tools were small:
                   a needle of moonlight
                   a spool of patience
                   and a single promise: "i will not rush you."

                 the maker listened to the stone for seven years
                 until the stone confessed its hidden note.

                       stone-note:  h u m
                       (not heard, but *felt* in the wrist)

                 then the maker built the bell around the hum,
                 not to trap it—just to give it a shape it could share.

                 when the bell finally rang, the stone changed:
                 it remembered it had once been a mountain
                 and before that, a seabed
                 and before that, a star that cooled into silence.

                 the bell rang again, and the silence learned a word:
                 "together."

                 every time the bell rang, one unspoken thing surfaced
                 into the world above—tiny, bright, survivable.

                 a laugh returned to a mouth that had forgotten it.
                 a letter found its way into a hand.
                 a forgiveness crossed a room like warm bread.
